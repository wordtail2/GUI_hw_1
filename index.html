German short haired pointer / Poodle mix (GSP)

Appearance

GSPs are medium to large-sized dogs, with a muscular, athletic build. They have short, dense coats that come in a variety of colors including liver, black, and white. Their coats are easy to maintain, and require only occasional brushing. They have a distinctive head shape with long, floppy ears and a strong jaw.

Temperment

GSPs are highly energetic and require a lot of exercise. They are intelligent and eager to please, making them easy to train. They are known for their loyalty and affection towards their owners, and can be excellent family dogs. However, they can be reserved around strangers, and may require socialization training to be comfortable around unfamiliar people and dogs.

Health

GSPs are generally healthy dogs, with a life expectancy of 10-12 years. However, like all breeds, they are prone to certain health conditions. Some of the most common health issues that affect GSPs include hip dysplasia, bloat, and epilepsy. Regular veterinary checkups, a healthy diet, and regular exercise can help prevent and manage these conditions.

Training

GSPs are highly trainable, and excel in obedience and agility training. They require a lot of mental and physical stimulation to prevent boredom, and benefit from activities like running, swimming, and hunting. Positive reinforcement training methods are recommended for GSPs, as they can be sensitive to harsh punishment.
